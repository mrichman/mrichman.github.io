--- layout: post title: Thoughts on Value-Based Fees for Agile Software Projects categories: - consulting - value-based-fees tags: - consulting - value-based-fees status: publish type: post published: true alias: /2010/01/12/thoughts-on-value-based-fees-for-agile-software-projects/index.html meta: _edit_last: '1' dsq_thread_id: '' --- Here's a quandary - we, as value-based IT consultants, aspire to charge value-based fees for our services (how obvious!). We know that doing so requires tight scope control. As such, this often requires a detailed specification. However, this is the very waterfall model that the agile movement shuns. How do you reconcile scope control, customer satisfaction, and ever-changing requirements with value-based fees? I don't think it's preferable - or practical - to do two week iterations on a value-based fee basis. Just as much as value-based fees benefit the client by not requiring them to make an investment decision every time they wish to engage you, they also may trap the consultant into having a value discussion/negotiation with the client for each iteration. Thoughts?
